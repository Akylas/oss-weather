# Astronomy Data

Sun and moon information for photographers, astronomers, and outdoor enthusiasts.

## Overview

OSS Weather provides comprehensive astronomy data including sun and moon positions, rise/set times, phases, and special events.

## Sun Data

### Sun Position

- **Real-time position** - Current sun location in the sky
- **Sun path visualization** - Arc showing sun movement
- **Altitude and azimuth** - Precise position coordinates

### Sun Times

- **Sunrise** - When the sun rises above the horizon
- **Sunset** - When the sun sets below the horizon
- **Solar Noon** - When the sun reaches its highest point
- **Day Length** - Total daylight hours

### Twilight Periods

- **Civil Twilight** - Bright enough to see without artificial light
- **Nautical Twilight** - Horizon visible for navigation
- **Astronomical Twilight** - Dark enough for astronomy

### Golden Hour & Blue Hour

- **Golden Hour** - Best time for photography (warm light)
  - Morning: After sunrise
  - Evening: Before sunset
- **Blue Hour** - Deep blue sky (great for cityscapes)
  - Morning: Before sunrise
  - Evening: After sunset

## Moon Data

### Moon Position

- **Current position** - Where the moon is in the sky
- **Moon path** - Arc showing moon movement
- **Altitude and azimuth** - Position coordinates

### Moon Times

- **Moonrise** - When the moon rises
- **Moonset** - When the moon sets
- **Moon Transit** - When the moon is highest

### Moon Phase

- **Current Phase** - Visual representation
- **Phase Name** - New, crescent, quarter, gibbous, full
- **Illumination** - Percentage of moon lit
- **Phase Calendar** - Upcoming phases

### Lunar Cycle

- **Days to Full Moon**
- **Days to New Moon**
- **Days since New Moon**

## Special Events

### Eclipses

When available:
- **Solar Eclipses** - Moon blocks sun
- **Lunar Eclipses** - Earth's shadow on moon
- **Visibility** - Whether visible from your location
- **Timing** - When eclipse occurs

### Blue Moon

Rare occurrence of second full moon in a month.

### Supermoon

When the moon is closest to Earth (appears larger).

## Using Astronomy Data

### Photography

**Golden Hour Photography**:
1. Check golden hour times
2. Arrive 15-30 minutes early
3. Best light quality for landscapes

**Astrophotography**:
1. Check moon phase (new moon best)
2. Verify astronomical twilight
3. Clear sky conditions

**Moon Photography**:
1. Check moonrise time
2. Look for high moon position
3. Consider phase (full moon impressive)

### Outdoor Activities

**Hiking**:
- Plan around sunrise/sunset
- Use twilight for extended daylight
- Consider moon illumination for night hikes

**Camping**:
- Moon phase affects darkness
- Sunrise/sunset for planning
- Day length for activities

**Fishing**:
- Many fish are active during moon phases
- Sunrise/sunset are prime times
- Check tidal effects (if coastal)

### Astronomy

**Star Gazing**:
- New moon best (darkest sky)
- Check astronomical twilight end
- Verify cloud cover

**Planet Viewing**:
- Check clear sky
- Note twilight times
- Moon phase consideration
